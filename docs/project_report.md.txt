# Healthcare Appointment Intelligence Platform

## Abstract

Healthcare organizations frequently face operational challenges due to missed patient appointments. These missed appointments result in resource wastage, scheduling inefficiencies, and reduced quality of healthcare services. This project analyzes healthcare appointment data to identify patterns associated with patient no-shows and generate actionable insights for hospital management.

The project utilizes SQL, Python, and data analytics techniques to perform data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and business intelligence reporting. A cleaned analytical dataset was created to support accurate decision-making and future predictive modeling.

---

# 1. Introduction

Patient no-shows represent a significant challenge in healthcare systems worldwide. Understanding the factors influencing appointment attendance can help healthcare providers improve scheduling efficiency and optimize resource allocation.

This project aims to analyze appointment records and identify key factors contributing to missed appointments.

# Day 3 Analysis

## Age Group Analysis

The 36–60 age group accounted for the highest number of appointments (37,761).

Young adults (19–35) exhibited the highest no-show rate at 23.83%, while patients aged 60+ demonstrated the lowest no-show rate at 15.20%.

## Gender Analysis

Female patients accounted for the majority of appointments. However, the no-show rate difference between male and female patients was minimal, indicating gender is not a strong predictor of attendance behavior.

## Scholarship Analysis

Patients enrolled in scholarship programs exhibited a higher no-show rate (23.74%) compared to non-scholarship patients (19.81%).

This suggests socioeconomic conditions may influence appointment adherence.

## Geographic Analysis

Jardim Camburi, Maria Ortiz, Itararé, Resistência, and Centro recorded the highest numbers of missed appointments.

These neighborhoods may benefit from targeted healthcare engagement initiatives.
